About MAHI Africa

Who We Are

A first-mover organisation dedicated to elderly health equity in conflict-affected Northern Nigeria, building the evidence base and community systems that did not exist before.

Our Story

Born at the Bedside

MAHI Africa was founded in 2025 by Dr. Philemon Kojo Woode during his housemanship at Federal Teaching Hospital Katsina. Month after month, more than 80% of deaths in the Medicine Department were attributable to preventable complications of undetected and uncontrolled NCDs in elderly patients.

These were not deaths caused by incurable disease. They were deaths caused by a system that was not designed for its ageing population. A system where elderly persons were told to go home and die rather than pursue treatment. Where there were no geriatricians, no screening programmes, no community health workers trained in elderly care.

MAHI Africa was created to change that — starting with Katsina, scaling to Nigeria, reaching the continent.

The Founder

Dr. Philemon Oritsetsolayemi Kojo Woode holds an MBBS from the University of Abuja (2024) and a BSc Biochemistry from Delta State University (2011). He is currently completing housemanship at Federal Teaching Hospital Katsina, where MAHI Africa was born.

As FAMSA President (2021–2022), he represented African medical students at the 71st and 72nd WHO African Region Regional Committee sessions. It was at the 71st session, where WHO Regional Director Dr. Moeti spoke on Africa's critical shortage of geriatricians, that his commitment to elderly health equity was crystallised.

He was selected from over 31,000 applicants across 41 African countries for the Tech4Africans AI and Machine Learning Scholar Cohort 6, with a 95% fee waiver. He holds five AI certifications through Microsoft Elevate, PoliSync, and TechCrush.

He is Principal Investigator on five active research studies on geriatric health, and has been recognised as Best House Officer in the Department of Surgery and Outstanding House Officer by the Association of Resident Doctors at FTHK.

Our Programs

Where We Work

Four interlocking pillars of intervention, grounded in community-level delivery and designed for the realities of conflict-affected Northwest Nigeria.

I
🏘️

Community Outreach and Screening

Direct engagement with elderly populations in rural and conflict-affected communities. MAHI Africa teams deploy to community health centres to conduct blood pressure screening, blood glucose testing, and structured health education for elderly men and women.

Our inaugural outreach in Batagarawa screened 88 elderly persons for hypertension and diabetes — the majority for the first time in their lives. This is the proof of concept that the full programme builds on.

Blood Pressure Screening Glucose Testing Health Education
II
🎓

Health Worker Training and Capacity Building

Training of community health workers and primary healthcare staff in geriatric-focused clinical skills, elderly communication, and NCD identification in older adults. Training is adapted for low-resource, high-insecurity settings.

CHWs become the sustainable long-term link between elderly communities and the health system, conducting regular home visits and monitoring patients who cannot travel to facilities.

CHW Training Geriatric Skills Home Visits
III
🩺

Donation of Medical Supplies and Equipment

Targeted donation of essential diagnostic and therapeutic supplies to facilities serving elderly populations. The inaugural donation to Comprehensive Health Centre Batagarawa included five sphygmomanometers, two AccuCheck glucometers, drugs, and consumables.

All donations are paired with a tracking system to ensure equipment reaches elderly patients rather than remaining unused in storerooms.

Sphygmomanometers Glucometers Drug Supply
IV
🌍

Rural-Proofing and Community Adaptation

Every MAHI Africa protocol, tool, training manual, and digital system is designed for the realities of conflict-affected Northern Nigeria: unreliable electricity, insecurity, limited literacy, cultural norms around ageing, and absence of referral pathways.

No intervention is considered viable unless it can function in a community where a CHW visit may be an elderly person's only healthcare contact in a given month.

Low-Resource Design Conflict Settings Cultural Adaptation
Flagship Project

Digital Community Hypertension Management

The HTN Treatment in Nigeria programme demonstrated treatment rates above 90% and BP control above 50% among 21,000 patients using structured primary care. MAHI Africa extends this proven model specifically to elderly populations in conflict-affected communities where no equivalent programme currently exists.

Months 1–3

Establish and Baseline

App development, CHW training, baseline data collection in Batagarawa. Stakeholder engagement with Katsina State PHC Authority. Target: 100 elderly persons enrolled, CHWs certified, app operational.

Months 4–6

Expand and Deepen

Expand to 2 additional communities. Pharmaceutical partnerships for drug supply. Second CHW training cohort. Target: 300 elderly persons enrolled across 3 communities.

Months 7–12

Measure and Advocate

Monitor BP control rates and referral completion. Analyse data. Produce policy brief. Present to Katsina State Ministry of Health. Target: 40% improvement in BP control rates.
